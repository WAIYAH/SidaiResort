Sidai Resort & Hotel, Full Review 2026: Is It Really the Best Resort in Narok County?

By Narok Travel Guide | Updated April 2026 | 10 min read

Verdict Upfront

★★★★★ 5/5

Yes. Sidai Resort & Hotel is a full five-star experience in Naroosura. Here is why this Loita Hills destination continues to lead Narok County hospitality in 2026.

View Sidai Resort

Arrival & First Impressions

The approach into Naroosura shifts quickly from transit mindset to destination calm. Sidai Resort is set along the Loita Hills slope where temperatures are cooler, the grounds are greener, and the pace slows down in a good way. First check-in interactions are warm and organized, exactly what a travel-weary guest needs.

The Rooms, 11 Maasai Sanctuaries Reviewed

Sidai's 11 named rooms are simple, practical and comfortable. The design focus is not flashy luxury, it is restful sleep, cleanliness, and connection to place. From Emirishoi to Elalai, each room feels rooted in local identity while covering the essentials expected from a quality resort stay.

The Pool, Size, Cleanliness and Day Pass Value

The pool is one of Sidai Resort's strongest differentiators. It is full-sized, clean and usable for both residents and day guests. In this part of Narok County, that alone makes Sidai a clear standout for families, groups and weekend travellers.

The Food, Breakfast to Nyama Choma

Dining is consistent across breakfast, lunch and dinner, with a menu that balances familiar staples and regional favorites. The nyama choma is the headline item, and for good reason. Portions are satisfying, flavor is strong, and pricing remains competitive for the overall quality.

The Events, Enchula and Entumo Halls

For organizations and private celebrations, Sidai's two halls are a major advantage. Enchula and Entumo support conferences, training sessions, weddings and social events with proper space planning and service support. This gives Sidai dual value as both leisure and business destination.

The Experiences, Bonfires, Birdwatching, Sundowners, Farm Visits

Sidai goes beyond room-and-meal hospitality. Guests can enjoy evening bonfires, birdwatching around the Loita ecosystem, farm experiences and sunset balcony sessions that make the stay feel immersive rather than transactional.

Staff & Service, Genuine Maasai Hospitality

Service quality is where Sidai earns its name most directly. Teams are approachable, responsive and culturally grounded. The result is an atmosphere that feels personal without being intrusive.

Value, Pricing Versus Experience

Rooms starting around KSh 1,000 and meal options from around KSh 100 keep Sidai accessible across budgets. Compared with what guests receive, location, amenities, hospitality, and activities, value-for-money is excellent.

Getting There

From Nairobi, use the Narok route, then continue from Narok Town to Naroosura (about 35km). For local routing and arrival support, contact Sidai directly: 0703 761 951 / 0721 940 823.
